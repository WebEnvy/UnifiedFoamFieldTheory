**1. Introduction**

The Diosi-Penrose model predicts a decoherence rate that increases with
local gravitational field strength. We examine the opposite prediction:
if local vacuum density decreases near massive objects, the rate of
vacuum-induced decoherence decreases near mass. The two models differ by
a sign in the gravitational correction term. That sign difference is
experimentally distinguishable.
