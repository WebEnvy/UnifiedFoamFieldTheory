## Gravity (A Push, Not a Pull)

You've been told gravity is a pull. An invisible force reaching across space, dragging things together. Newton imagined it. Einstein refined it. But neither of them told you *why*.

Here's why:

When mass is present, the foam around it gets slightly less dense. Not by much — a few parts per billion near Earth's surface. But the foam *behind* you is denser than the foam between you and the Earth. And denser foam pushes harder.

You're not being pulled down. You're being pushed down by the pressure difference.

**Gravity is a push, not a pull.**

The formula comes out exactly right. Start with the foam density gradient:

- Higher density (higher pressure) far from mass
- Lower density (lower pressure) near mass
- Pressure difference creates net force

The result: Φ = -GM/r

That's Newton's gravitational potential, derived from foam pressure. No mysterious action at a distance. No invisible pulling force. Just pressure.
