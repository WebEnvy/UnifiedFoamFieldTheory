1. Gravity as a Foam Pressure Gradient

Standard physics describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime produced by mass. Within UFFT, gravity has a different physical picture. The vacuum is a structured medium at baseline density ρ₀. Near a massive object, the foam is displaced and local vacuum density decreases:

ρvac(r) = ρ₀ · (1 - 2GM/rc²) (1)

Near mass: less foam, lower pressure. Far from mass: more foam, higher pressure. An object sits in this pressure gradient and is pushed toward the low-pressure region near the mass. That push is what we experience as gravity.

Gravity is not a force pulling you down. It is the foam pushing you toward where there is less foam to resist. To move a large object, you do not overpower gravity mechanically. You locally modify the foam pressure gradient around it. The foam then does the work.
