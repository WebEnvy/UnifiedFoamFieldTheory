Void-Pair Conservation as the Physical Mechanism of Quantum Entanglement

Luke Martin

Independent Researcher | 2026

Abstract

We propose that quantum entanglement is the physical manifestation of void-pair conservation in a quantized vacuum foam. A displacement event D --- the creation of one bubble and one complementary void --- is the fundamental ontological object. The bubble and void are two addresses of one event, not two correlated objects. This model escapes Bell's theorem because D is inherently non-local and resists the factorization Bell's proof requires. The antipodal symmetry of the void-pair selects the quantum singlet state, which produces the experimentally confirmed correlation E(a,b) = -cos(theta). A testable prediction for three-particle connected foam topologies is proposed.

1. Void-Pair Conservation and Bell's Theorem

Axiom Zero: B(x) + V(x') = D. D cannot be factored into local parts at x and x'. Bell's theorem requires the correlation function to be writable as:

E(a,b) = integral A(a,lambda)*B(b,lambda)*rho(lambda)*d(lambda)
(1)

where lambda is a local hidden variable. The displacement event D is not local --- it is defined by its extension between both endpoints simultaneously. The factorization cannot be applied. The foam escapes Bell not by finding a flaw in it but by providing an ontological category that Bell's proof was not designed to test.

Numerical confirmation: a classical local hidden variable model with antipodal structure gives a triangular correlation function E_LHV = 1 - 2*theta/pi, differing from -cos(theta) by up to 0.207. This is the experimentally ruled-out region. The foam model uses the quantum singlet, not the classical approximation.

2. The Singlet State from Antipodal Symmetry

The void is the antipodal complement of the bubble in every direction. The unique two-particle state satisfying perfect anti-correlation in every measurement direction simultaneously:

|D> = (1/sqrt(2)) * (|up,down> - |down,up>)
(2)

Selected by symmetry, not assigned arbitrarily. From it:

E(a,b) = <D|(sigma_a x sigma_b)|D> = -cos(theta_ab)
(3)

✓ Evaluated at 0°, 45°, 90°, 135°, 180° --- matches quantum mechanical prediction exactly.

3. Testable Prediction

For three particles created from a connected three-way foam topology, void-pair conservation predicts correlations differing from the standard GHZ prediction. Testable with existing multi-photon entanglement experiments --- a specific experimental preparation not yet performed.

4. Conclusion

The void-pair model provides the physical reason quantum entangled states are non-separable: they describe one object --- the displacement event D --- not two correlated objects. The singlet state is selected by the void-pair's antipodal symmetry. The correct quantum correlations follow. The question quantum mechanics answers is what are the correlations. The void-pair model answers what is the physical thing being described.
