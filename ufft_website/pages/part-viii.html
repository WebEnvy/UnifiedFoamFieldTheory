Gravitational Suppression of Quantum Decoherence via Variable Vacuum Foam Density

Luke Martin

Independent Researcher | 2026

Abstract

We present a derivation of quantum decoherence rates within a vacuum pressure gradient model of gravity, in which local vacuum density decreases near massive objects according to rho_vac(r) = rho_0(1 - 2GM/rc²). This model predicts that decoherence rates are suppressed near massive objects --- in direct opposition to the Diosi-Penrose (DP) model. The sign reversal in the gravitational correction term produces a measurable difference of 3GM/rc² in decoherence rate ratios at two gravitational potentials. We propose a concrete experimental protocol using atomic interferometry at varying gravitational potentials, achievable with existing space-based platforms, that can discriminate between the two models.*

1. Introduction

The Diosi-Penrose model predicts a decoherence rate that increases with local gravitational field strength. We examine the opposite prediction: if local vacuum density decreases near massive objects, the rate of vacuum-induced decoherence decreases near mass. The two models differ by a sign in the gravitational correction term. That sign difference is experimentally distinguishable.

2. Theoretical Framework

2.1 Vacuum Density

rho_vac(r) = rho_0 * (1 - 2GM/(r*c^2))
(1)

Factor of 2 verified: rho_vac = 0 at r = r_s = 2GM/c². Consistent with Schwarzschild metric.

2.2 Position-Dependent Decoherence Rate

Gamma(r) = Gamma_0 * (1 - 2GM/(r*c^2))
(2) 
tau(r) = tau_0 / (1 - 2GM/(r*c^2))
(3)

2.3 Comparison with Diosi-Penrose

Gamma_VD(r)/Gamma_VD(inf) = 1 - 2GM/(r*c^2)
[This model: suppressed near mass] 
Gamma_DP(r)/Gamma_DP(inf) = 1 + GM/(r*c^2)
[Diosi-Penrose: enhanced near mass]

The difference between model predictions is 3GM/rc² --- a sign difference observable with next-generation space atom interferometers.

3. Experimental Protocol

Measure decoherence time tau at gravitational potential phi_1. Repeat at phi_2. Compute the ratio R = tau(r_2)/tau(r_1) = (1 - 2*phi_1)/(1 - 2*phi_2). This model predicts R > 1 for phi_2 < phi_1 (higher altitude = longer coherence). Diosi-Penrose predicts R < 1. One model must be wrong. Effect magnitude at Earth surface versus ISS: approximately 8 × 10⁻¹¹. Within reach of BECCAL and STE-QUEST platforms.

4. Conclusion

The vacuum foam density model predicts gravitational suppression of decoherence --- opposite in sign to Diosi-Penrose. The sign difference is unambiguous and forms the basis of a clean experimental discrimination using existing space-based atom interferometry technology.
