Definitions and Conventions

Key Terms Used Throughout This Work

Foam (Vacuum Foam)

The quantized Planck-scale structure of the vacuum. Not empty space, but a medium composed of discrete units at the Planck scale (lP = 1.616 × 10⁻³⁵ m). Each unit is termed a "bubble." The foam has baseline density ρ₀ = 5.155 × 10⁹⁶ kg/m³ and baseline pressure P₀ = 4.633 × 10¹¹³ Pa.

Bubble

A single quantized unit of the vacuum foam. Bubbles are not particles but structural units of the medium. Their density determines local vacuum properties.

Void

The complementary absence created when a bubble is displaced. Voids are genuine emptiness — regions where foam density is reduced or absent. The void is not a mathematical abstraction but a physical structure with measurable consequences.

Void-Pair (Axiom Zero)

The fundamental conservation law of the theory. Every displacement event D creates exactly one bubble B at position x and one complementary void V at position x':

B(x) + V(x') = D
Neither exists without the other. This is the single axiom from which entanglement, the singlet state, and Bell correlation escape derive.

Displacement Event (D)

The fundamental object in UFFT. Not a particle, not a wave, but a topological event in the foam that creates both a bubble and a void simultaneously. Entangled particles are two addresses of one displacement event.

Imprint

The record of a measurement outcome written into the foam structure. When a quantum system interacts with its environment, the foam records which state was realized. This imprint is what causes decoherence — the loss of quantum superposition.

Emergent Layer

The macroscopic scale at which foam dynamics average to produce linear physics. At scales much larger than the Planck length, the foam's non-linear behavior averages out, producing Newtonian gravity, Maxwell electromagnetism, and linear quantum mechanics. This is not an approximation error — it is the correct description of foam physics as observed from within the emergent layer.

Methodology

How This Theory Was Developed and Verified

Research Process

This work was developed through iterative dialogue between the author (Luke Martin) and Claude (Anthropic), an AI assistant. The development process spanned 18 sessions over approximately three weeks in February–March 2026.

Role Contributions
Human Author (Luke Martin) All theoretical ideas, physical intuitions, and conceptual frameworks. Direction of inquiry and identification of which questions to pursue. Final judgment on which claims are supported and which remain open.
AI Assistant (Claude) Numerical verification of all equations. Dimensional analysis to check unit consistency. Identification of connections to existing mathematical literature. Document preparation and formatting. Independent audit of claims (finding errors that required correction).

Verification Standards

Every quantitative claim in this document was subjected to the following checks:

1. Dimensional Analysis

Each equation was verified to have consistent units on both sides.

Left side:  [Pa] = [kg/(m·s²)]
Right side: [kg/m³] × [m²/s²] = [kg/(m·s²)] ✓

Key predictions were computed explicitly.

3. Consistency Check

Each new result was checked against prior results and established physics. The factor-of-two error in the original gravity derivation was caught by this process.

4. Boundary Conditions

Each equation was evaluated at limiting cases (r → ∞, r → rs, etc.) to verify physically sensible behavior.
